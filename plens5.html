SP05 - Reserves and Resource Classification and Reporting: Responding to a Changing World

Monday 16th June, 16:00-17:30

Whilst there have been substantial changes in the regulations for reporting reserves and resources, there have also been substantial changes regarding the location and nature of petroleum resources. The shift to a growing dependence on unconventional resources creates challenges and opportunities for classification systems.  Over the last decades this has led to a gradual migration from the initial approach of classifying resources in the ground to classifying investment projects by what they can produce.  Regulatory and standard setting bodies with national and multinational scopes, professional societies and associations, global organizations and industry have all contributed to update the earlier classification systems to reflect advances in technology and to ensure credibility in reserves and resource reporting, but has enough been done?  The energy industry additionally lives in an increasingly carbon-constrained world; one response has been for a number of oil and gas companies to grow their alternative energy activities.  What would the benefits be for the sector to have a classification system that could apply to both renewable and non-renewable energy projects?

EW02 - Uruguay NC: Petroleum habitats and new exploration frontiers in the south Atlantic Basin

Tuesday 17th June, 12:00-13:00

Even though exploration for oil and natural gas has been conducted for over half a century in both sides of the South Atlantic, huge new discoveries are still been made as new petroleum systems have been uncovered, particularly in deeper waters. The huge pre-salt discoveries made offshore South East Brazil and more recently Angola, the Upper Cretaceous and Tertiary accumulations found on both ends of the Equatorial Margin and the renewed interest in the Southern South Atlantic Basins offshore Uruguay and Namibia make this region one of the most promising new oil frontiers in the world. However, the exploration risks and challenges cannot be under estimated, and understanding the various petroleum systems on both sides of the Atlantic, how they evolved and correlate, is critical to the industry success. The findings of this workshop were summarized and presented during a Special Session at the 21st World Petroleum Congress in Moscow.

MS09 - Pakistan - Ministerial Session

Thursday 19th June, 10:45-11:45

Pakistan’s growing energy needs, driven mainly by the industrial and transport sectors are projected to double by 2028. In this scenario, current oil and gas production is, and will clearly remain, insufficient to cater to incremental demand. The Government of Pakistan’s primary goal is to overcome present and future energy deficit in the country by bridging the demand-supply gap through a combination of inorganic and organic growth, with particular focus on expansion of Exploration and Production (E&P) activities. Though future discoveries in accessible and mature basins of South and Central Asia are expected to partly replace depleting reserves and declining production over the next 10 years, new sustainable reserves would have to accrue primarily from frontier and under-explored and offshore basins together with exploitation of unconventional resources. Success in these areas would provide the necessary momentum towards ensuring long-term energy security and offsetting significantly prevalent reserves depletion levels. The current Petroleum Policy has enabled a competitive regulatory and pricing framework, offering US$7 to US$9 for offshore gas besides a US$1 bonanza on the first three discoveries. In a bid to achieve a diverse hydrocarbons mix, the Government has also introduced separate price regimes for tight, low BTU and stranded gas for the first time and is developing a regulatory framework for shale gas pilots. To incentivize E&P activity, the Government awarded 50 exploration blocks in the country earlier this year through competitive bidding and is expected to announce another bid round for onshore and offshore blocks in the near future. The Government invites potential partners to invest in Pakistan’s oil and gas sector, sharing latest technological know-how, skilled human resources and experience, particularly in exploitation of offshore and unconventional reserves, for strategic multilateral business collaboration to mutual benefit.
